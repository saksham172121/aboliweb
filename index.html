01
Revenue Initiative

Marketing Coordinator
Global Reach Sydney · 2024

Identifying and launching an OVHC revenue stream during a difficult visa environment.

2024 was a challenging year for the international education sector. Changes in Australian immigration laws led to increased visa rejections and declining enrolments — and Global Reach needed to respond. During a team strategy session, I raised the idea of expanding into Overseas Visitor Health Cover (OVHC) — a product adjacent to our existing OSHC offering, targeting students who had completed their studies and were transitioning to post-study visas.

My manager and Managing Director backed the proposal and asked me to help lead it. I coordinated new contracts with insurance partners, developed campaign strategies to reach our existing onshore student database, and mapped out the promotional channels we'd use. The project required commercial thinking, not just marketing execution — I had to understand the revenue model, the compliance angle, and the communication challenge simultaneously.

The initiative successfully added a new service line to the business, diversifying revenue at a time when the core business was under pressure. It also deepened my understanding of B2B partnership management, stakeholder negotiation, and the intersection of policy, product, and communication.

02
Event Strategy

FAM Tour Coordinator
Global Reach Sydney · 2022–Present

Coordinating the annual FAM Tour — a flagship stakeholder experience across Australian universities.

Global Reach's annual Familiarisation Tour (FAM Tour) is one of its most strategically important activities. Selected employees travel to partner universities across Australia to build first-hand knowledge of campuses, facilities, and academic culture — and to strengthen the relationships that underpin Global Reach's university partnerships and commissions.

Together with my manager, Ms. Minakshi Basak, I coordinate the full lifecycle of this event: communicating with universities about participant profiles, preparing employee briefings, managing accommodation and travel logistics, scheduling sessions, and overseeing on-the-day time management. I also brief universities on what training and presentations to prepare for the visiting team.

I've been actively involved in this initiative for three consecutive years. It's taught me that events at this level are fundamentally relationship-management exercises — and that the real outcome isn't the tour itself, but the trust it generates with partners who could enrol hundreds of students through our pipeline.

03
Outreach Marketing

Outreach Marketing Coordinator
Global Reach Pune · 2021–2022

Marketing study abroad to students who look exactly like I did two years earlier.

My first professional role at Global Reach in Pune wasn't a coincidence — it made perfect sense. I had just spent a year pursuing an overseas scholarship myself, which meant I understood the fears, the motivations, and the information gaps that every prospective international student faces. I wasn't selling an abstract product. I was describing a path I had personally walked.

I visited schools and colleges, conducted group seminars, and worked one-on-one with students and families who were considering studying overseas. My ability to speak from personal experience — not just from a company brochure — built an immediate kind of trust that conventional marketing rarely achieves. I wasn't a salesperson. I was a proof of concept.

This role taught me something I carry into every marketing project: the most powerful communication is the kind that makes the audience feel understood, not targeted. That insight shapes how I write copy, how I plan campaigns, and how I think about brand.

Typography
Display / Headlines — Cormorant Garamond Light

Editorial. Warm. Intelligent. Use Light (300) for all headlines and pull quotes. Free on Google Fonts.

Body / UI / Labels — DM Sans Regular

Clean, modern, and never distracting. Use Regular (400) for body copy and Medium (500) for labels and small caps. Free on Google Fonts. Never use bold in body text — it breaks the editorial feel.

Photography & imagery direction

Avoid stock photography wherever possible. Use your own photos — Sydney locations, candid moments, places you've worked or visited on FAM Tours. If you need placeholder imagery, look for editorial-style photography with natural light, neutral tones, and real environments. Avoid corporate headshots; opt for a natural, well-lit portrait. If you commission a headshot, ask the photographer for a "journalist portrait" rather than a "LinkedIn photo" — the brief will produce a completely different result.
